    Education

    HIVE has adapted a slum area called Bharathi nagar in Tamil Nadu, India. On November 2016, HIVE collected field data to understand and analyze the needs and the demands of this vulnerable community; furthermore interventions were strategically planned to serve this community. This slum is known for its high prevalence of illiteracy rate, low socio-economic status, poor infrastructure and poor quality of life. On January 2017, HIVE founded a community welfare center at Bharathi nagar.

    Educational Services rendered to Children

    • Free education, tuition, library and study hall
    • Workshops and sessions for healthy life style adaptation
    • Trainings in extra-curricular activities including sports, arts and culture
    • Providing yearly free educational materials, books, bags, stationeries etc.
    • Providing school tuition fees to the deserving candidates.

    Educational Services rendered to Women

    • Free trainings on Occupational skills: Tailoring, Handcrafts
    • Workshops on lifestyle enhancement
    • Education on finance management/ parenting techniques/ problem-solving skills/ home-making skills
    • Creating Self-help groups to enhance emotional sustainability and financial stability
    • Providing monthly food provisions to poverty-stricken families
    • Promoting meditation and self-care
    • Providing psychotherapeutic counseling to women and adolescent fighting depression and other mental ailments.

    Apart from this slum area, HIVE carters’ similar services to different downtrodden communities. Several educational awareness programs are being conducted in schools, colleges, slum groups, fisherman community, drug/ substance abusers. Evidence based approaches are used and volunteering professionals are involved in providing quality enriched workshops and sessions. Several educational materials (books, notebooks, stationery, awareness enhancing materials, brochures, pamphlets etc) are being distributed free of cost state-wide. Our Follow up reviews has shown significant increase in knowledge of our vulnerable beneficiaries. As Benjamin Franklin once said, “An investment in knowledge pays the best interest”. We believe that, this aggregate knowledge will in turn aid in making this world a better place for our future generation.

    Health Care Services

    HIV/AIDS and TB afflicted

    For the past 16 years, HIVE has been actively involved in providing support and health care to HIV/ AIDS and TB positive patients. Several awareness programs has been conducted in schools, colleges and public gatherings as an initiative to eradicate the externalized stigma around HIV/AIDS and TB. Support group meetings are conducted every month. We provide them with health advice and consultations, we motivate drug adherence and provide ART and ATT referrals. Professional mental health care is offered, meditation and other recreational programs are rendered. Monthly food provisions (Inclusive of nutrition enriched diet to improve CD4 counts) are supplied. HIVE always covers the travel expense and lunch of each beneficery. Annual meet is organized each year where extracurricular activities are performed, and each individual is gifted with new clothes and food, provision. As our founder believes “self love and acceptance are the keys to quality enhanced life years” and this has been the central dogma of HIVE

    Health care camps

    Similar support group meeting customized for visually impaired people is being conducted at Thirukovilur. In a remote village of Peranampet, HIVE supports abandoned old aged people by providing food and clothing. Homeless senior citizens are referred to old age homes. HIVE envisions establishing an old age home for this vast geriatric community. Balanadhi, a remote hill station with no schools or hospitals has caught HIVE’s interest. Women support group meetings and tuition for children is being organized, plans to establish community welfare centre with free health care and education access to all is at progress. Free Medical and Dental camps (free medicines and referrals to higher centre) are executed at different locations. Mobile health care camps are being conducted in remote rural areas of Tamilnadu.

    Facing Pandemic Crisis

    During dengue epidemic, HIVE visited several slums houses and delivered advices on prevention strategies to mitigate the spread of disease. We also distributed free mosquito-repellents to families. Awareness programs on vector-borne diseases like Malaria/ Dengue was provided as well. Following that was COVID-19 pandemic outbreak. HIVE actively enrolled itself during lock-down crisis. Providing basic necessities to the underprivileged, supplying medical and protective gears, assisting with awareness campaigns on sensitization and social distancing are some of our rendered services. We are looking forward to collaborate with the government and fight Covid in the days to come.

    PAST MISSIONS

    Shelter homes

    In the year 2005, HIVE established a shelter home to elevate the lifestyle and socio economic status of HIV affected women, stigmatized by the community, family and self. Called as Cornerstone project, this program was dedicated to equip women with self confidence in their personal and career life, to provide with temporary accommodation, food, medication along with moral and emotional support, to promote leadership qualities and to improve their skills in career and social life. In the year 2006, HIVE established an orphanage called HIVE Blossoms. This project started to help enhance the growth and healthy lifestyle of downtrodden, orphan, and children of terminally ill parents. Around 65 children were provided with quality education (Private English-medium schools) and eminent livelihood. In the year 2013, both orphanages were shut down due to lack of financial and human resources.
